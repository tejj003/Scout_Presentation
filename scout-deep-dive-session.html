Session design

Teach one repeatable way of working.

Do not demo every feature. Teach the pattern people can reuse tomorrow: ask clearly, ground the work, let Scout act, review the output, and iterate.

Mental model

The Scout loop: brief, ground, act, verify.

1

Brief

State the job, audience, constraints, and what a good result should look like.

2

Ground

Give Scout the files, chats, screenshots, meetings, or repo context it needs.

3

Act

Let it draft, edit, browse, automate, summarize, build, or run checks.

4

Verify

Review the output, ask for evidence, and approve only the actions you trust.

Special feature · M365 + WorkIQ

Scout helps when work context is scattered.

Use it to prepare for meetings, triage mail, find documents, understand people/context, and convert scattered signals into a clear next action.

Teaching prompt: Prepare me for my next meeting. Use relevant mail, chats, docs, and calendar context. Separate facts, decisions, risks, and questions I should ask.

Special feature · Coding and files

For code and files, Scout should work like a careful teammate.

The teaching point: ask it to explore locally, preserve unrelated work, make focused edits, run the right validation, and summarize the result.

Find the owner

Trace the behavior to the file, function, test, or command that controls it.

Change narrowly

Use the repo's style, avoid unrelated cleanup, and keep the edit easy to review.

Run evidence

Prefer a focused test, build, typecheck, lint, or browser check over guesswork.

Explain the delta

Report what changed, what passed, and what risk remains.

Live demo flow

Teach the pattern while you demo the feature.

The audience should be able to repeat the method after the session: brief the task, ground it in context, let Scout act, verify, then approve or refine.

Guardrails

The more Scout can do, the more deliberate the operator must be.

Grant only needed access

Give file, shell, browser, and M365 access intentionally for the job at hand.

Preview what leaves

Review email, Teams, calendar, and other outbound content before it is sent.

Protect sensitive context

Keep private schedules, mail, files, and confidential details out of public output.

Ask for explanations

Have Scout say what it did, what it used, and where uncertainty remains.

Require evidence

Use previews, tests, screenshots, diffs, source links, or tool results as proof.

Keep judgment human

Humans approve risky, external, sensitive, or irreversible steps.

Starter prompts

Give people prompts that teach the operating model.

Co-create

Use these notes and files to create a clear teaching artifact for [audience]. Start with the narrative, then make the output polished and easy to present.

Automation

Design an automation for [recurring task]. Define the trigger, what Scout should check, what it should draft, and what must wait for my approval.

M365 prep

Prepare me for [meeting/topic] using relevant mail, chats, files, and calendar context. Separate facts, decisions, risks, owners, and questions.

Engineering

Find where [behavior] is implemented, explain the local code path, make the smallest safe change, and run the focused validation.

Takeaway

Teach people to bring Scout work, not just questions.

The strongest use cases combine context, tools, artifact creation, verification, and human judgment. That is where Scout becomes more than chat.
